Dog Collar

I worked for a small startup developing an AI Dog Collar that would respond to the owner‚Äôs commands in the pet‚Äôs voice. Inside the main body of the band, there was a custom-designed PCB with ESP32-C6 and STM32 microprocessors, sensors, speakers, battery monitor, and debugging peripherals. The device recorded sounds from the pet‚Äôs environment through a MEMS microphone into a PSRAM buffer, passed it through a low-pass filter with Automatic Gain Control, and transferred the 8-bit audio from the STM32 to ESP32 through the 40MHz SPI interface, which uploaded it to the cloud over a TCP socket connection. We redefined the WIFI drivers in the Zephyr RTOS library to send AT-commands to the ESP32. The hardest part about the project was keeping the power consumption low while time-sharing between WIFI and BLE operations. We were able to extend the tiny battery life to multiple days.
